Things to Know

  1. Historical Significance:

    Travnik has a rich history and was the capital of the Ottoman province of Bosnia for almost 150 years. Explore historical sites such as the Travnik Fortress and the colorful houses in the Old Town.

  2. Bosnian Cuisine:

    Try local Bosnian dishes like cevapi (grilled minced meat), burek (filled pastry), and sogan-dolma (stuffed onions). Bosnian coffee is also a must-try.

  3. Climate:

    Travnik experiences a continental climate with warm summers and cold winters. Be prepared for varying weather conditions depending on the time of year.

  4. Language:

    The official languages are Bosnian, Croatian, and Serbian. English might be spoken in tourist areas, but learning a few basic phrases in the local language can be helpful and appreciated.

  5. Currency:

    The official currency is the Bosnian Convertible Mark (BAM). Ensure you have some local currency for small purchases, as not all places may accept credit cards.

  6. Local Customs:

    Bosnian people are known for their hospitality. It's customary to greet people with a friendly "Dobar dan" (Good day) and "Hvala" (Thank you) for politeness.

  7. Transportation:

    Travnik is well-connected by road. If you're arriving by public transportation, the bus station is a common point of entry. Taxis are also available for local transport.

  8. Cultural Etiquette:

    Respect local customs and traditions. When visiting religious sites, dress modestly, and ask before taking photographs. It's also polite to remove your shoes when entering someone's home.

  9. Safety:

    Travnik is generally a safe destination, but it's always advisable to take basic safety precautions. Keep an eye on your belongings and be cautious in crowded areas.

  10. Tourist Attractions:

    Apart from historical sites, explore the colorful Old Bazaar, visit the Blue Water Springs (Plava Voda), and take in the panoramic views from the Old Town.
